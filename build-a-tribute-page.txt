  <h4>Timeline of Nelson Mandela's Life</h4>
  <ul>
<p>July 18, 1918 – Born in Mvezo, South Africa.</p>  
<p>1925– Began primary school, where his teacher gave him the English name "Nelson."</p>  
<p>1934 – Underwent the traditional Xhosa initiation ceremony into adulthood.</p>  
<p>1939– Enrolled at the University of Fort Hare but was later expelled for participating in a student protest.</p>  
<p>1941– Moved to Johannesburg and worked in various jobs while studying law.</p>  
<p>1943 – Joined the African National Congress (ANC) and became involved in anti-apartheid activism.</p>  
</p>1944 – Co-founded the ANC Youth League. Married Evelyn Mase (divorced in 1958).</p>  
<p>1952 – Launched the Defiance Campaign against apartheid; opened South Africa’s first Black law firm with Oliver Tambo.</p>  
<p>1956 – Arrested and charged with treason along with 155 others; acquitted in 1961.</p>  
<p>1958 – Married Winnie Madikizela (divorced in 1996).</p>  
<p>1961 – Helped form the ANC's armed wing, Umkhonto we Sizwe, after realizing peaceful resistance was not enough.</p>  
<p>August 5, 1962 – Arrested and sentenced to five years in prison.</p>  
<p>June 12, 1964 – Sentenced to life imprisonment for sabotage and conspiracy against the government (Rivonia Trial).</p>  
<p>1964–1982 – Imprisoned on Robben Island, performing hard labor.</p>  
<p>1982 – Transferred to Pollsmoor Prison.</p>  
<p>February 11, 1990 – Released from prison after 27 years.</p>  
<p>1991 – Elected president of the ANC.</p>  
<p>April 27, 1994 – South Africa held its first multiracial elections. Mandela was elected the country’s first Black president.</p>  
<p>May 10, 1994 – Inaugurated as President of South Africa.  
<p>1996 – Divorced Winnie Madikizela-Mandela.</p>  
<p>1999 – Stepped down after one term as president.</p>  
<p>2004 – Retired from public life.  
<p>July 18, 2009 – The UN declared "Nelson Mandela International Day" to honor his legacy.</p>  
<p>December 5, 2013 – Passed away at age 95.</p>
</ul>
